The Site Now Has an RSS Feed

Short and sweet: this website now has an RSS feed. If you would like to follow what I publish without checking the site by hand, you can now subscribe to it in your favourite feed reader.

The feed lives at: 

        https://federicobruzzone.github.io/feed.xml

It is a single, combined feed: it gathers blog posts, scientific publications, preprints, and activities (talks, contributions, milestones) into one chronological stream, newest first. Every item carries a category tag, so your reader can tell a post apart from a paper or an activity at a glance.

How to subscribe. Paste the URL above into any RSS reader (for example NetNewsWire, Feedly, or Inoreader). Most readers will also discover the feed automatically if you simply give them the address of the site — the pages now advertise it through a <link rel="alternate" type="application/rss+xml"> tag in their <head>. You will also find a small RSS icon in the footer of every page.

On the technical side, the feed is generated by a tiny, dependency-free script straight from the existing HTML pages, and a GitHub Action rebuilds it automatically whenever new content is pushed. So from now on, whenever something new appears on the site, it will show up in the feed without any extra effort.

Happy reading! 📡
